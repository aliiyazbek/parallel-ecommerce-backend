## Defence layers
- **Layer 1 — DRF ScopedRateThrottle** (5/min per user on `/api/coupons/redeem/`). Protects against a single user flooding an endpoint. Over-quota requests return **HTTP 429** before touching business logic.
- **Layer 2 — `threading.BoundedSemaphore`** (bulkhead). Caps the total number of simultaneous heavy operations. Excess callers wait up to a short timeout and fail fast with **HTTP 503** instead of oversubscribing a finite resource.

## Layer 2 — Bounded concurrency cap

Each scenario launches **30** threads at a finite resource pool of **10** slots. Scenario A hits the pool directly; Scenario B goes through `@limit_concurrency(max_concurrent=3, timeout=2.0s)`.

| Scenario | Threads | Wall (ms) | OK | Pool-exhausted | HTTP 503 | p50 total | p95 total | p50 wait | p95 wait |
|----------|--------:|----------:|---:|---------------:|---------:|----------:|----------:|---------:|---------:|
| SCENARIO A  —  WITHOUT cap   (30 ops, pool=10) | 30 | 205 | 10 | 20 | 0 | 201 | 201 | 0 | 0 |
| SCENARIO B  —  WITH cap=3  (30 ops, pool=10, timeout=2.0s) | 30 | 2019 | 30 | 0 | 0 | 1103 | 2005 | 902 | 1805 |

## Interpretation
- **Without cap**: every thread races to the pool at once. The pool fills, the overflow fails with *pool-exhausted*. Fast but **unreliable under load**.
- **With cap**: the semaphore admits only N callers at a time. Excess traffic queues briefly; nothing oversubscribes the pool. Slightly higher tail latency, **100% success**.

- Applied: `orders/views.py::CheckoutView.post` (`@limit_concurrency(max_concurrent=3, timeout=2.0)`)
